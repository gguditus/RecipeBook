Chicken Francese

This is a recipe for the identity crisis that is Chicken Francese. The dish is Italian-American in origin, but butchered French in name. Despite this, it is a favorite in the area surrounding its suspected site of origin, Rochester, New York. Myself and everyone I know like it with extra lemon in the sauce, and this recipe is a reflection of that.
Sick of chicken? Substitute veal (possibly the origin of this dish)!
Can't eat meat? Substitute butterflied shrimp, scallops, sole, or any white fish sliced thin!

  • 4 Chicken breasts sliced into cutlets
  • 1/4 cup oil (canola, peanut, or similar to fry)
  • 8 ounces of spinach
  • 1 clove of garlic, sliced
  • 1 tbsp of lemon juice
  • 1 tsp of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of chicken stock
  • 1 pound thin spaghetti or angel hair or (for a maximum deliciousness meal) homemade fettuccine, link to that here

  • Batter
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 3-4 eggs
  • 2 lemons zested and juiced

  • Sauce
  • 5 lemons juiced and 3 zested
  • 1 lemon sliced (optional)
  • 3-4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups dry white wine
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 tbsp butter
  1. Cut or butterfly chicken breasts in half to make chicken cutlets, about 1/4 inch thick (if too thick, cover with saran wrap and pound with a flat mallet or heavy pan to flatten).
  2. Measure 1/2 cup of flour and add to a shallow pie dish, or a plate. Add salt, pepper, garlic powder, and zest of two lemons to the flour and mix. Beat 3-4 eggs in a second shallow pie dish or plate, then add the juice of two lemons and mix.
  3. Add oil to a large shallow pan and heat over medium heat and heat an oven to 350* F.
  4. Slice a clove of garlic and add it, the spinach, the 1 tsp of olive oil, 1 tbsp of chicken stock, and 1 tbsp of lemon juice to a pan and saute, or to a bowl and cook in a microwave.
  5. Dredge the chicken cutlets in the seasoned flour, dip the floured chicken into the egg mixture, and add straight to the heated pan.
  6. After about 2-3 minutes, flip and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until both sides are golden-brown and crispy.
  7. Meanwhile, mince the cloves of garlic, then zest and juice the remaining lemons.
  8. Once all chicken is cooked, add it to a tray and bake at 350* F until the thickest pieces reach 150* F.
  9. In the same pan, melt the butter and add the minced garlic. Cook the garlic over medium heat until fragrant, about 1 minute*. Then add 2 tbsp of flour slowly while mixing the butter to make a roux. Cook about another minute.

    10. *If you want, you can add some slices of lemon here to add even more lemon to the sauce and act as a nice garnish for the finished dish. Once seared, remove and reserve for later.

  10. Slowly add the dry white wine to the pan and allow the alcohol to cook off and reduce while scraping any browned bits in the bottom of the pan.
  11. Next add the remaining lemon juice and lemon zest to the sauce. Finaly, add 3-4 cups of chicken broth until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. If you add too much, you can simply reduce by cooking the sauce a bit longer or adding a bit more butter (as long as your sauce does not break). If you reduce the sauce too much, you can add a bit of water to make the sauce more liquid.

    12. If desired, add the chicken back to the sauce (I recommend it).

  12. Finally, serve by plating spaghetti, then spinach, then chicken, optionally the sliced lemons here, and finally, smother with sauce!
